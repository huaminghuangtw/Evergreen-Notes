# Impress Yourself

> “What other people think of me is none of my business.” — Wayne Dyer

**The Courage to Be Disliked** — The philosophical stance, rooted in Adlerian psychology, that interpersonal freedom comes from separating your tasks from others’ tasks. You cannot control how others feel about you—only how you feel about yourself.

Stop people-pleasing. Everyone thinking you’re great means nothing, if you don’t think you’re great. Be impressive to others is overrated. <mark>Be impressive to yourself. You don’t need to impress anyone but yourself. You become inhumanly strong when you try to impress only one person: YOU.</mark> You are always going to win the “YOU” game. The only person you have to compete against is yourself. You learn the most when there is nobody to cheer you but yourself.

When your standard of evaluation moves from _external_ (what do others think?) to _internal_ (am I proud of this?), several things happen:

1. **Your energy becomes undiluted** — You stop second-guessing every action based on imagined judgment.
2. **Your decisions become faster** — The question shifts from “What will they think?” to “Is this right for me?”
3. **Your resilience increases** — Rejection no longer feels like a verdict on your worth. It is simply information.
4. **Your growth accelerates** — You take risks you would have avoided if you needed an audience’s approval.
