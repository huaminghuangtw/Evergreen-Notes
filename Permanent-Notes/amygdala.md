= 杏仁核

The amygdala evaluates sensory information for emotional processing, especially potential threats, and triggers appropriate responses (e.g., fight-or-flight).

It is most famous for its role in generating and regulating [fear](fear.md) and [anxiety](stress-and-anxiety.md).

The amygdala is also implicated in [aggressive and defensive behaviors](anger.md).

---

Chronic stress can cause the amygdala to become overactive, which may:

* Heighten emotional reactivity and anxiety
* Impair rational [decision-making](decision-making.md) by weakening the **prefrontal cortex**
* Contribute to mental health issues such as depression, PTSD, and anxiety disorders

---

* Practices like [meditation](meditation.md), [walking](walk.md), and grounding techniques can help reduce amygdala activity and lower stress/anxiety levels.
* Exposure to feared situations can help retrain the amygdala and reduce fear responses. [Nothing diminishes anxiety faster than action](stress-and-anxiety.md).
* [Emotional regulation strategies](emotion-control.md) and [reframing fear as information](a-true-transformation-begins-with-a-mental-shift.md) are effective for managing amygdala-driven responses.
