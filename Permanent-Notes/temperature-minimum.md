---
title: Temperature minimum
modified: 2026-05-01
---

The temperature minimum is the lowest point of core body temperature within a 24-hour cycle. This nadir typically occurs just before the body prepares to wake [^1]. Because core body temperature is tightly regulated by the [circadian rhythm](circadian-rhythm.md), the timing of this minimum provides a reliable indicator/anchor point/reference point of an individual’s internal biological night.

---

Understanding your temperature minimum is crucial for anyone seeking to adjust their [sleep](sleep.md) schedule or recover from [jet lag](jet-lag.md).

Suppose you usually wake up at 7:00 AM. Your temperature minimum likely occurs between 5:00 and 5:30 AM. If you want to shift your sleep schedule earlier, you would seek bright light exposure after this time. Conversely, to shift later, you would expose yourself to light before this minimum.

---

To estimate your temperature minimum, record your core body temperature immediately upon waking for 3–5 consecutive days. Average these readings to approximate the nadir.

[^1]: To be more specific, 90 minutes to 2 hours **before** your average waking time.
