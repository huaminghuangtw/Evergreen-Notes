---
title: Collective Unconscious
modified: 2026-06-10
---

The **collective unconscious**, a concept by **Carl Jung**, refers to a universal layer of the unconscious mind shared by all humans. Unlike personal unconsciousness, which is based on individual experiences, the collective unconscious contains **inherited, universal elements** such as **archetypes**—recurring symbols and themes like the Hero, the Mother, or the Shadow—that appear across cultures in myths, dreams, and art. These archetypes are **not learned but innate/inborn**, shaping how people perceive and interpret the world on a deep, often unconscious level.
